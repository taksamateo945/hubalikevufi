    There are currently 3 seasons and 151 episodes of Bluey, the popular Australian animated children’s television series. Bluey’s popularity has since extended beyond its native Australia, finding fans around the world who appreciate its positivity, humor, and relatability.

    The first season was released in 2018 and the second season followed in 2020, both have 52 episodes. The third season, which premiered in 2021, has so far released 47 episodes, making it a total of 151 episodes.

    How Many Seasons of Bluey are There?

    Currently, only three seasons of Bluey have been released. The show, created by Joe Brumm, has garnered immense popularity for its relatable characters, heartwarming stories, and valuable life lessons for both children and adults. Below are details of each season of Bluey:

    Season 1 (2018)

    The inaugural season of Bluey premiered in 2018. Comprising 52 episodes, this season introduced viewers to the titular Blue Heeler puppy, Bluey, and her adventures with her family and friends. Through imaginative play, valuable life lessons, and relatable situations, the show quickly gained a devoted following.

    Season 2 (2020)

    Building on the success of the first season, Bluey returned with its second season in 2020. Like its predecessor, the second season also featured 52 episodes. The show continued to captivate audiences with its endearing characters, meaningful themes, and the endearing bond between Bluey, her younger sister Bingo, and their parents, Bandit and Chilli.

    Season 3 (2021–23)

    The third season of Bluey made its debut in 2021. This ongoing season has been releasing episodes, with each installment adding to the charm of the show. As of now, the third season has aired 47 episodes, each contributing to the legacy of Bluey and further enriching the stories of its characters.

    How Many Episodes of Bluey Are There?

    Bluey has delighted audiences with a total of 151 episodes spread across three seasons.

    Season 1 (52 episodes)

    The inaugural season of Bluey showcased 52 episodes, offering a diverse array of heartwarming stories and valuable life lessons. This season was thoughtfully divided into two parts, each containing 26 episodes.

    • Part 1: Aired from October 1, 2018, to October 26, 2018, this initial batch of episodes introduced viewers to the charming world of Bluey, laying the foundation for the show’s popularity.
    • Part 2: Following a brief hiatus, the second part of the first season returned with episodes airing from April 1, 2019, to December 12, 2019. This continuation of the season further solidified the show’s appeal and endearing characters.

    Season 2 (52 episodes)

    The second season of Bluey mirrored the structure of the first, encompassing 52 episodes divided into two parts.

    • Part 1: From March 17, 2020, to April 11, 2020, the first part of the second season graced screens, bringing back the beloved characters and their relatable adventures.
    • Part 2: The second part of the season began airing on October 25, 2020, and ended on April 4, 2021, solidifying the show’s reputation for heartwarming narratives and meaningful insights.

    Season 3 (47 episodes)

    The third and ongoing season of Bluey has so far offered a total of 47 episodes, aired in three different parts.

    • Part 1: From September 5, 2021, to December 16, 2021, the first part of the third season debuted, once again inviting viewers into the world of Bluey, Bingo, and their endearing family.
    • Part 2: Airing from June 13, 2022, to June 23, 2022, the second part of the season continued to captivate audiences with its heartwarming tales.
    • Part 3: The third part of the third season, comprising 10 episodes, aired from April 9, 2023, to June 11, 2023, further enriching the tapestry of Bluey’s storytelling.

    • Banned Bluey Episodes

    There are no episodes of Bluey that have been totally banned. However, there have been some episodes that have been censored or altered in certain regions. In the United States, some episodes of Bluey have been censored for a variety of reasons and they include:

    • The use of fart jokes
    • The depiction of childbirth
    • The use of a racially insensitive term
    • The depiction of body image issues

    These issues revolve around sensitivities related to language, themes, and potential impact on young audiences. It is important to note that the censorship of Bluey is not universal. In other regions, such as Australia, the episodes are aired uncensored.

    Season 1

    During the first season, a few episodes underwent changes, though none were completely banned in the U.S. Notable alterations include:

    • “Daddy Robot”: This episode and a couple of others were censored due to the use of a racially insensitive term. The term was replaced with a harmless substitute to ensure cultural sensitivity.
    • “Fruit Bat”: A scene in this episode was cut from U.K. and U.S. broadcasts due to safety concerns, as it depicted a potentially dangerous activity on a wet tiled floor.
    • “Taxi”: The U.S. version of this episode ends earlier than the original, omitting a scene involving pretend vomiting.
    • “Markets”: The version aired on Disney+ in the U.S. excludes a scene involving unicorn droppings.

    Season 2

    • “Dad Baby”: This episode, centered around pregnancy and childbirth, was banned in the U.S., as it involved Bandit simulating giving birth.
    • “Army”: The episode underwent a minor alteration, changing dialogue to be more inclusive and sensitive.
    • “Trains”: In the U.S., a scene involving imaginary poop was changed to a slug.
    • “Flat Pack”: Apart from the term alteration seen in Season 1, this episode’s theme sparked conversations about evolution and the afterlife.

    Season 3

    • “Exercise”: A portion of this episode was edited out globally due to concerns of fat-shaming and body dysmorphia.
    • “Perfect”: Dialogue alterations were made to address mature topics more suitable for young viewers.
    • “Born Yesterday” and Others: Instances of potentially sensitive language, including the word “groin,” were censored or altered in the U.S. version.

    When Did Bluey Come Out?

    Bluey premiered on the Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC) in Australia on October 1, 2018. It was created by Joe Brumm and is produced by a Queensland-based company, Ludo Studio. The show has been a huge hit in Australia, and it has also been gaining popularity in the United States. It is currently available to stream on Disney+.
