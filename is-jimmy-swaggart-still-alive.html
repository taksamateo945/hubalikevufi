    Career Beginnings

    Swaggart and his wife Frances, whom he married when they were both teenagers, lived in poverty in the 1950s while Swaggart travelled the countryside of rural Louisiana preaching.

    Jimmy Swaggart Televangelism

    Jimmy Swaggart Televangelism In Baton Rouge, Louisiana, Swaggart started airing “Camp Meeting Hour,” a once-weekly 30-minute Christian webcast.

    Additionally, he bought the neighbourhood AM radio station WLUX, which he used to air gospel music, sermons, and other programming. In 1975, Swaggart increased the length of his weekly television programme to an hour.

    Later, he produced “A Study in the World,” a second television programme airing during the week, as well as a weekend programme featuring a church service.

    When his shows were frequently broadcast on more than 3,000 stations, Swaggart’s fame reached its zenith in the 1980s.

    Later in his career, Swaggart established the SonLife Broadcasting Network, a round-the-clock cable and satellite television network where he serves as host of the daily Bible study programme “The Message of the Cross.”

    In 1984, Swaggart founded Jimmy Swaggart Bible College, which at initially offered degrees in communication and education.
