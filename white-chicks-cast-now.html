    White Chicks, a comedy movie that was released in 2004, was well received by the public but poorly received by critics. The Wayans brothers, who also wrote the script for this show, Marlon and Shawn, were featured in Keenen Ivory Wayans’s direction. In White Chicks, two FBI agents, Marcus and Kevin, are tasked with escorting two young heiresses of a billionaire to a weekend-long fashion event in The Hamptons in order to safeguard them from potential kidnappings after failing in prior missions. Unfortunately, they have a car accident en there, and when the girls see the small wounds on their faces, they decide to skip the event. The agents decide to pose as them for the duration of the weekend out of a desire to avoid failing a mission again and a concern for losing their employment. However, it will be far more difficult than they anticipate to get into these girls’ social circles.

    Although White Chicks received negative reviews from critics and even received five nominations for the Golden Raspberry Awards, it nevertheless managed to earn $113 million worldwide and develop a devoted following that helped the movie become a cult favourite. A sequel to the movie was planned for release five years after its first release, but it was ultimately scrapped. As actor Terry Crews made a suggestion that a potential sequel was being discussed in recent years, rumours about this new project started to surface once more. The rumours haven’t been formally confirmed, though, as of yet. Let’s look at the cast members of White Chicks’ most recent endeavours as we wait for any updates on this conceivable sequel.

    Brittany Daniel

    The third member of the Wilson sisters’ adversarial trio, Megan Vandergeld, is Brittany Daniel. Following her performance in White Chicks, the actress was recast opposite the Wayans brothers in the 2006 movie Little Man, but her involvement in projects decreased significantly after that. Her most recent productions to date include the 2015 film Joe Dirt 2: Beautiful Loser and the 2022 remake of Cheaper by the Dozen starring Gabrielle Union and Zach Braff. Her most recent television appearances were on The Game and in a supporting role on Kenya Barris’ ABC sitcom Black-ish.

    Busy Philipps

    Following her appearance as Karen Googlestein in White Chicks, Busy Phillipps was cast in a variety of supporting roles until landing one of her most well-known roles to date as Laurie Keller in the sitcom Cougar Town in 2009. The Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series went to Phillipps for that performance. 2018 was a significant year for her career as well. She hosted her own television programme, Busy Tonight, which ran till 2019. On the other hand, she was the author of the book This Will Probably Hurt a Little. Phillipps recently voiced Foofy Dog in the animated films I Feel Pretty and DC League of Super-Pets. She currently has a starring role in Girls5eva, a third-season Netflix transfer of a Peacock series.

    Frankie Faison

    Frankie Faison was well-known internationally before appearing in White Chicks thanks to his portrayal of Barney Matthews in the Hannibal Lecter series. The actor has been very active over the years, appearing in numerous films and television shows. He has recently acted in movies including Fatherhood, I’m Your Woman, and Till, a biography that also st ars Danielle Deadwyler, Jalyn Hall, Haley Bennett, and Whoopi Goldberg. He also appeared in the inaugural season of The Rookie: Feds in 2022, a spin-off of The Rookie that debuted in September. There hasn’t been any information on a potential show renewal up to this point.

    Jaime King

    In the movie White Chicks, Heather Vandergeld, a rival of the Wilson sisters, is portrayed by actress and model Jaime King. King has worked on numerous projects over the years, especially films, and has devoted most of her attention to her acting profession. Her most notable recent films were Code Name Banshee and Escape Plan 2: Hades, Out of Death, in which she acted alongside Bruce Willis. She also starred in the two-season Z Nation spinoff Black Summer. She will reportedly act opposite Frank Grillo, Mekhi Phifer, Dermot Mulroney, and Scott Adkins in the upcoming thriller Lights Out in 2023.

    Jennifer Carpenter

    Jennifer Carpenter, a Sony Pictures Releasing actress, is best known for her role as Debra Morgan in the Showtime series Dexter, which aired from 2006 to 2013, for which she won a Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Television Series. Dexter lasted from 2006 to 2013. Carpenter played the same character again in the one-season miniseries Dexter: New Blood. She has been in a number of movies recently, but her most notable role was as the voice actress in the Mortal Kombat video games Mortal Kombat Legends: Scorpion’s Revenge and Mortal Kombat Legends: Battle of the Realms. Alongside Amanda Seyfried, she also made an appearance in Amy Koppelman’s A Mouthful of Air. Carpenter portrayed Lisa Anderson in White Chicks.

    Jessica Cauffiel

    Jessica Cauffiel was well recognised for her supporting role as Margot in Legally Blonde, the 2001 rom-com starring Reese Witherspoon, before she was cast as Tori Wilson in White Chicks. Following her work on the Wayans’ movie, Cauffiel occasionally went on other projects, although she gradually reduced the number of appearances she made in the business. She last appeared in a movie in 2006’s Hoot, which also starred Brie Larson, Logan Lerman, and Luke Wilson. She is anticipated to reprise her role as Margot in Legally Blonde 3, despite her absence from the film industry; the film’s release date has not yet been disclosed.

    Lochlyn Munro

    Over the years, Lochlyn Munro has worked on a variety of film and television productions. His most well-known recent works are the superhero show Peacemaker, which has a single season on HBO Max, and the teen drama Riverdale, where he played Hal Cooper until the show’s sixth season. Bruce Willis and Munro co-starred in Cosmic Sin in 2021, and in the Detective Knight trilogy, which concluded in January with the release of Detective Knight: Independence, they appeared in a number of other recent movies.

    Marlon Wayans

    Marlon Wayans portrayed Anthony Copeland II, who took on the role of Tiffany Wilson at the Hamptons fashion show, in the movie. Even though Marlon frequently appeared in productions with his brother, he continued to work in the business by taking on little parts in many movies and television shows over the years. He starred in The Curse of Bridge Hollow, a Netflix comedy costarring Priah Ferguson, Kelly Rowland, and John Michael Higgins, and produced Respect, a biopic about Aretha Franklin’s life that starred Jennifer Hudson, in 2021. Additionally, Wayans appeared on television as the host of the programmes Oh Hell No! with Marlon Wayans and Marlon Wayans Presents: The Headliners. He is anticipated to have an appearance in Ben Affleck’s 2023 film Air, which chronicles the founding of Nike and how the corporation signed a contract with Michael Jordan.

    Shawn Wayans

    In this comedy, Shawn Wayans co-stars with his brother as Kevin Copeland, the agent who impersonated Brittany Wilson. This actor, writer, and producer primarily collaborated with Marlon throughout his career on movies including Don’t Be a Menace, Scary Movie and its first sequel, Little Man, and Dance Flick. The Wayans Bros., a sitcom that debuted in 1995 and ran on The WB until 1999 when it was cancelled due to declining ratings, starring both brothers. A brief cameo appearance in the 2016 animated series Animals was Wayans’ most recent professional engagement. His return to the big screen might possibly be signalled by the White Chicks sequel.

    Terry Crews

    Terry Crews’ portrayal of Latrell Spencer in White Chicks was one of his first significant roles following his NFL retirement in 1997. This actor and former American football player has a varied career that has allowed him to work on many different types of projects over the years. Beginning in 2013, Crews played Terry Jeffords in Brooklyn Nine-Nine, a role he kept up until the show’s conclusion in 2021. He also acted in movies including John Henry, Deadpool 2, and The Willoughbys, a Netflix animated movie that got him nominated for a Critics’ Choice Super Award for Best Voice Actor in an Animated Movie. As of 2019, Crews is the show’s host. She previously held the same position on the programmes America’s Got Talent: The Champions and America’s Got Talent: Extreme.
