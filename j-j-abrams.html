    J.J. Abrams Biography

    Jeffrey Jacob Abrams was born in New York on June 27, 1966. Abrams’ parents are executive producer Carol Ann Abrams and television producer Gerald W. Abrams. Abrams’ father was a producer of made-for-television movies, and the younger Abrams followed in his footsteps by creating short Super-8 films as a child. At the age of 16, Abrams earned his first work in the film business, scoring the low-budget science-fiction picture Nightbeast (1982).

    He attended Palisades High School. After high school, Abrams planned to attend art school rather than a traditional college, but she instead enrolled at Sarah Lawrence College.

    Career

    Abrams and Jill Mazursky collaborated on a script that became the 1990 comedy “Taking Care of Business.” The film was a flop at the box office.

    In 1992, he wrote the screenplay for the science fiction romance “Forever Young,” starring Mel Gibson. The film was well-received by the audience. He made his producing debut with the romantic comedy “The Pallbearer” in 1996.

    In 1998, he co-created his first television show, “Felicity,” with Matt Reeves. The show focused on the experiences of the main character Felicity, a university student. It lasted four years.

    Abrams created the action series “Alias,” which was about the life and work of a fictitious CIA operative called Sydney Bristow and the aliases she used to conceal her actual job from her friends and family. Five seasons were televised from 2001 to 2006.

    He co-produced and directed the critically acclaimed science fiction picture “Star Trek” in 2009. It was also financially successful.

    He worked on the production of “Mission: Impossible – Ghost Protocol” with Bryan Burk and Tom Cruise in 2011. It went on to become the Mission Impossible film with the most box office revenues.
